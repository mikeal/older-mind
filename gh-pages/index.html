Mind Manual

This is a collection of teachings regarding meditation and the processes Mind appears to be functioning in.

This is intended as a "practice manual" taken up by the author and shared freely with others.

It is not a "book" in the traditional sense, but a collection of teachings and practices that can be used to deepen one's understanding of Mind written in HTML. Being in HTML, these resources are not limited.

This work is ongoing, as continuous iteration is the result of continued practice.

This work follows a format found often in Buddhism in which teachings are relayed in numbered symmetric pairs. As such, the first teach is on one topic "Buddha," the second on two topics which are themselves pairs of two, and so on for each consecutive topic.

  1. Buddha
  2. Entering Meditation
    1. Shamatha & Vipassana
    2. Vitarka & Vicara
  3. Meditation wrt Suffering
    1. Craving(Taṇhā): gratification, existence, non-existence
    2. Poisons: Greed, Hatred, Delusion
    3. Disorders: Wind, Coldness, Dampness
  4. Meditation that ends Suffering
    1. The Four Foundations of Mindfulness
    2. The Four Immeasurables
    3. The Four Jhanas
    4. The Four Formless Realms
  5. Meditation wrt Enlightenment
    1. The Five Precepts
    2. The Five Aggregates
    3. The Five Powers
    4. The Five Destinations
    5. The Five Tathagatas
  6. The Boddhisattva Discipline
    1. The Six Doors
    2. The Six Perfections
    3. The Six Realms
    4. The Six Persceptions
    5. The Six Sense Bases
    6. The Six Elements
