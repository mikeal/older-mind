Aṅguttara Nikāya 5.27 Numerated Discourses 5.27 3. Pañcaṅgikavagga 3. The Section of Five Factors Samādhisutta The Discourse on Concentration “Samādhiṁ, bhikkhave, bhāvetha appamāṇaṁ nipakā patissatā. "Practice immersion (samādhi), O monks, that is limitless, with subtle mindful awareness. Samādhiṁ, bhikkhave, bhāvayataṁ appamāṇaṁ nipakānaṁ patissatānaṁ pañca ñāṇāni paccattaññeva uppajjanti. For those of you cultivating such immersion (samādhi), limitless, with with subtle mindful awarenes, five knowledges arise personally. Katamāni pañca? What are these five? ‘Ayaṁ samādhi paccuppannasukho ceva āyatiñca sukhavipāko’ti paccattaññeva ñāṇaṁ uppajjati, "Knowledge arises personally that this concentration leads to happiness now and results in future happiness, ‘ayaṁ samādhi ariyo nirāmiso’ti paccattaññeva ñāṇaṁ uppajjati, knowledge arises personally that this concentration is noble and taintless, ‘ayaṁ samādhi akāpurisasevito’ti paccattaññeva ñāṇaṁ uppajjati, Variant: akāpurisasevito’ti → mahāpurisasevitoti (mr) knowledge arises personally that this concentration is not practiced by the worthless but is the resort of the noble (Variant: not practiced by the worthless, indeed → frequented by the great man (mr)), ‘ayaṁ samādhi santo paṇīto paṭippassaddhaladdho ekodibhāvādhigato, na sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritagato’ti paccattaññeva ñāṇaṁ uppajjati, Variant: na sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritagato’ti → na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritacatoti (bj); na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritappattoti (sya-all); na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāritāvaṭoti (pts1ed); na ca sasaṅkhāraniggayhavārivāvaṭoti (mr); na sasaṅkhāraniggayhavāriyādhigatoti (?) knowledge arises personally that this concentration is peaceful, sublime, obtained by serene confidence, unification, without directing the mind to material existence" (Various readings indicate the phrasing related to not engaging in active volition), ‘sato kho panāhaṁ imaṁ samāpajjāmi sato vuṭṭhahāmī’ti paccattaññeva ñāṇaṁ uppajjati. Variant: sato vuṭṭhahāmī’ti → sato ca uṭṭhahāmīti (bj); sato vuṭṭhahāmīti (sya-all, km); so kho panāhaṁ imaṁ samādhiṁ satova samāpajjāmi satova vuṭṭhahāmīti (pts1ed) knowledge arises personally that 'mindfully I enter into and emerge from this concentration.' (Variants clarify the aspect of mindful entrance and emergence), Samādhiṁ, bhikkhave, bhāvetha appamāṇaṁ nipakā patissatā. Develop concentration, O monks, that is limitless, with unwavering attention, thorough awareness. Samādhiṁ, bhikkhave, bhāvayataṁ appamāṇaṁ nipakānaṁ patissatānaṁ imāni pañca ñāṇāni paccattaññeva uppajjantī”ti. For those of you cultivating such concentration, limitless, with unwavering attention and thorough awareness, these five knowledges arise personally." Sattamaṁ. Seventh.
