Great Section Jhānasutta Variant: Jhānasutta → jhānanissayasuttaṁ (bj) Depending on Absorbtions (Jhānas) “Paṭhamampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, jhānaṁ nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; dutiyampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, jhānaṁ nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; tatiyampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, jhānaṁ nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; catutthampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, jhānaṁ nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; ākāsānañcāyatanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; viññāṇañcāyatanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; ākiñcaññāyatanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; nevasaññānāsaññāyatanampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi; saññāvedayitanirodhampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmi. I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on the first absorbion. I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on the second absorbion. I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on the third absorbion. I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on the fourth absorbion. I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on ākāsānañcāyatanaṁ (base of infinite space). I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on viññāṇañcāyatanaṁ (base of infinite consciousness). I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on ākiñcaññāyatanaṁ (base of nothingness). I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on nevasaññānāsaññāyatanaṁ (base of neither perception nor non-perception). I, O Beggars, say that trauma disappears in dependence on the cessation of perceiving sensations. ‘Paṭhamampāhaṁ, bhikkhave, jhānaṁ nissāya āsavānaṁ khayaṁ vadāmī’ti, iti kho panetaṁ vuttaṁ. Kiñcetaṁ paṭicca vuttaṁ? Idha, bhikkhave, bhikkhu vivicceva kāmehi …pe… paṭhamaṁ jhānaṁ upasampajja viharati. So tehi dhammehi cittaṁ paṭivāpeti.
